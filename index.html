Research

Efficient Distribution Learning with Error Bounds in Wasserstein Distance
Eduardo Figueiredo, Steven Adams, Luca Laurenti
In review, 2026
arXiv

We develop a new algorithmic and theoretical framework to approximate an unknown probability distribution from finite samples while bounding the Wasserstein error with high confidence. The method combines optimal transport, nonlinear optimization, and concentration inequalities, and yields a tractable mixed-integer linear program whose size depends only on the support of the approximate distribution. Across benchmarks, the approach returns smaller supports and tighter bounds than comparable state-of-the-art methods.

Evaluating randomized smoothing as a defense against adversarial attacks in trajectory prediction
Julian F. Schumann, Eduardo Figueiredo, Frederik Baymler Mathiesen, Luca Laurenti, Jens Kober, Arkady Zgonnikov
IEEE International Conference on Intelligent Transportation Systems, 2026
arXiv

This work introduces and evaluates a randomized smoothing defense tailored to trajectory prediction models under adversarial perturbations. Extensive experiments show consistent robustness improvements across multiple base models and datasets. The method improves adversarial resilience without reducing non-adversarial prediction accuracy, while remaining simple and computationally inexpensive.
